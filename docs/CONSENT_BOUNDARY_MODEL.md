# Consent Boundary Model

Status: Sprint 13.5 enforcement contract

## Purpose

The consent boundary model turns Sprint 13 source-threat policy into a code-level contract that
classifiers can consume before content becomes durable profile support.

Implementation lives in `src/imprint/connectors/consent.py` and is consumed by
`RuleBasedArtifactClassifier`.

## Consent Classes

Private-source connectors must classify every normalized artifact into one consent class:

- `owner_authored` ‚Äî the profile subject authored the artifact.
- `third_party_authored` ‚Äî another person authored the artifact.
- `mixed` ‚Äî authorship is blended, quoted, forwarded, group, or multi-speaker.
- `unknown` ‚Äî authorship cannot be proven.
- `system_generated` ‚Äî generated by software, notification systems, templates, or assistants.

## Consent Actions

The consent boundary maps classes to actions:

- `allow` ‚Äî owner-authored content may proceed to normal classification and durable support checks.
- `exclude` ‚Äî third-party or system-generated content cannot support the profile.
- `quarantine` ‚Äî mixed or unknown content requires review and cannot become durable support by default.

The default source-specific rule is conservative: only `owner_authored` is allowed. Third-party and
system-generated content are excluded. Mixed and unknown content are quarantined.

## Source-Family Mapping

Sprint 13.5 defines concrete mappings for future connector families:

- Gmail / sent mail: `source_family: gmail`
- iMessage / chat: `source_family: imessage`
- Transcript / recorder: `source_family: transcript`
- Database / cloud rows: `source_family: database`

Legacy local synthetic adapters that do not provide private-source consent hints keep their existing
behavior. Future private-source connectors must provide consent hints; missing hints for explicitly
private connector families quarantine by default.

## Required Source Hints

Future connectors should provide one or more of:

- `consent_class`
- `source_family`
- `source_role`
- `participant_role`
- `speaker_role`
- `system_generated`
- `message_role`
- `group_chat`
- `mixed_speakers`

These hints are advisory but enforceable. Classification records the evaluated consent class, action,
and reason codes in `source_hints_considered`.

## Classifier Integration

The classifier applies consent after its existing authorship and contamination logic:

- `exclude` always downgrades the final label to `EXCLUDED`.
- `quarantine` downgrades included/quarantined content to `QUARANTINED` unless it was already excluded.
- `allow` preserves the normal classifier decision.

This ensures third-party received mail, group-chat participant content, and third-party transcript
speakers cannot become durable profile support without an explicit future policy change.

## Regression Coverage

Sprint 13.5 tests prove:

- received Gmail-style mail is excluded before durable support
- group chat participant content is quarantined
- third-party transcript speaker content is excluded
- owner-authored source hints can be allowed

Any future private connector must add source-specific consent fixture tests before implementation.
